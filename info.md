# The difference between Endangered species and Extinct species

| Endangered species                                                                                                           | Extinct species                                                        |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Animals/plants whose numbers are diminishing to a level that they might face extinction are known as the endangered animals. | Animals/plants that no more exist on earth are called extinct species. |
| Can be saved by efforts of conservation.                                                                                     | Cannot be revived.                                                     |
| Examples — African wild dog, Royal Bengal tiger                                                                              | Examples — Dodo, Dinosaurs                                             |

# Wildlife Sanctuary vs Biosphere Reserves vs National Parks

| Feature        | Biosphere Reserve                                                                                                                         | National Park                                                                            | Wildlife Sanctuary                                          |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary Focus  | Overall biodiversity, scientific research, and sustainable development                                                                    | Protecting extensive areas with significant ecological, natural, and historical features | Protecting a specific species or a group of species         |
| Zones          | Core (strict protection), Buffer (research, education, limited activity), and Transition (sustainable development with human communities) | No zones; human activity is heavily restricted or prohibited                             | No zones, but often has flexible boundaries                 |
| Human Activity | Permitted in buffer and transition zones, with restrictions                                                                               | Strictly regulated or prohibited, with limited exceptions                                | Permitted with some restrictions, such as regulated tourism |
| Management     | Internally managed with various zones, and internationally recognized by UNESCO                                                           | State or government-owned and managed                                                    | Can be government or privately owned                        |
| Size           | Often encompasses multiple national parks and sanctuaries                                                                                 | Varies from small to large; generally a significant area                                 | Generally smaller in size compared to national parks        |
